Understanding how we use cookies to enhance your experience

Comprehensive Cookie Policy

1. What Are Cookies?

Cookies are small text files that are placed on your computer or mobile device when you visit a website. They are widely used to make websites work more efficiently, as well as to provide information to the site owners.

Cookies typically don't contain much information except for a URL of the website that created the cookie, a unique random number, and an expiration date. They cannot be used to identify you personally and cannot contain viruses or malware. Cookies help websites remember your preferences and improve your browsing experience by tailoring content to your interests.

2. How We Use Cookies

We use cookies to improve your experience on our website by remembering your preferences, understanding how you use our site, and providing you with personalized content.

Our cookies help us analyze web traffic, identify which pages are being used, and help us improve our website by tailoring it to customer needs. We only use this information for statistical analysis purposes. Cookies allow us to provide you with a better website by enabling us to monitor which pages you find useful and which you do not.

3. Types of Cookies We Use

We use several types of cookies on our website:

  • Essential cookies: Necessary for the website to function properly
  • Performance cookies: Help us understand how visitors interact with our website
  • Functionality cookies: Allow the website to remember choices you make
  • Targeting cookies: Used to deliver content relevant to your interests
  • Third-party cookies: Set by services we use for analytics and advertising

4. Essential Cookies

These cookies are strictly necessary for the website to function and cannot be switched off in our systems. They are usually only set in response to actions made by you which amount to a request for services, such as setting your privacy preferences, logging in or filling in forms.

You can set your browser to block or alert you about these cookies, but some parts of the site will not then work. These cookies do not store any personally identifiable information. Without these cookies, services you have asked for cannot be provided, and we only use these cookies to provide you with those services.

5. Performance Cookies

These cookies allow us to count visits and traffic sources so we can measure and improve the performance of our site. They help us to know which pages are the most and least popular and see how visitors move around the site.

All information these cookies collect is aggregated and therefore anonymous. If you do not allow these cookies we will not know when you have visited our site, and will not be able to monitor its performance. These cookies help us improve the way our website works by ensuring users are finding what they are looking for easily.

6. Functionality Cookies

These cookies enable the website to provide enhanced functionality and personalization. They may be set by us or by third party providers whose services we have added to our pages.

If you do not allow these cookies then some or all of these services may not function properly. These cookies may collect personally identifiable information that you've disclosed, such as your username. They can also be used to remember changes you have made to text size, fonts and other parts of web pages that you can customize.

7. Targeting Cookies

These cookies may be set through our site by our advertising partners. They may be used by those companies to build a profile of your interests and show you relevant adverts on other sites.

They do not store direct personal information, but are based on uniquely identifying your browser and internet device. If you do not allow these cookies, you will experience less targeted advertising. These cookies may track your visits to other websites and share this information with other organizations such as advertisers.

8. First-Party Cookies

First-party cookies are set by the website you're visiting, and they can only be read by that site. We use first-party cookies for various functions including website analytics, remembering your preferences, and authentication.

These cookies are generally considered to be more secure than third-party cookies as they can't be accessed by other websites. We maintain strict control over all first-party cookies used on our site. First-party cookies are essential for providing the services you request from our website.

9. Third-Party Cookies

Third-party cookies are set by a domain other than the one you're visiting. We allow certain third-party cookies for services like Google Analytics, social media integrations, and embedded content from platforms like YouTube.

These cookies might track your usage of our site to help advertisers deliver more relevant ads or to limit how many times you see an ad. You can control these cookies through your browser settings. Third-party cookies are subject to the privacy policies of the companies that set them.

10. Session Cookies

Session cookies are temporary cookies that remain in your browser's cookie file until you leave the site. They enable you to carry information across pages of our site without having to re-enter information.

Session cookies are essential for the operation of our shopping cart and checkout process, as well as for maintaining your logged-in state as you navigate through our site. These cookies are deleted when you close your browser. Without session cookies, many website functions would not work properly.

11. Persistent Cookies

Persistent cookies remain on your device for a set period of time specified in the cookie. We use persistent cookies to remember your preferences when you return to our site.

These cookies allow us to recognize you when you return to our website and remember your preferences (such as your language preference). Persistent cookies can remain on your device for days, months or even years. They are used for functions like remembering login details so you don't have to enter them every time.

12. Cookie Duration

Different cookies have different lifespans. Session cookies expire when you close your browser. Persistent cookies have expiration dates set by the website.

Our persistent cookies typically expire after 30 days, though some may remain for up to 2 years. We regularly review the duration of our cookies to ensure we're not retaining data longer than necessary. The lifespan of a cookie depends on its purpose and the type of information it stores.

13. Cookie Consent

When you first visit our website, we display a cookie consent banner that allows you to accept or reject non-essential cookies. Your preferences are stored in a cookie so we don't have to ask you on every visit.

You can change your cookie preferences at any time by clicking the "Cookie Settings" link in our footer. Essential cookies cannot be disabled as they are necessary for basic website functionality. We comply with all applicable regulations regarding cookie consent and ensure that your privacy is respected.

You can set your browser to refuse cookies or to alert you when cookies are being sent. If you disable cookies, some features of our website may not function properly. For detailed instructions on how to manage cookies, please refer to your browser's help section.
